CALIFORNIA PUBLIC RECORDS ACT - COMPREHENSIVE STATUTORY PROVISIONS INCLUDING 2025 AMENDMENTS

SECTION 7921.000. LEGISLATIVE INTENT
In enacting this chapter, the Legislature, mindful of the right of individuals to privacy, finds and declares that access to information concerning the conduct of the people's business is a fundamental and necessary right of every person in this state.

CONSTITUTIONAL FOUNDATION (PROPOSITION 59, 2004):
"The people have the right of access to information concerning the conduct of the people's business, and, therefore, the meetings of public bodies and the writings of public officials and agencies shall be open to public scrutiny." (Cal. Const., art. I, ยง 3(b)(1))

SECTION 7922.000. DEFINITIONS

"Public records" includes any writing containing information relating to the conduct of the public's business prepared, owned, used, or retained by any state or local agency regardless of physical form or characteristics.

"Writing" means any handwriting, typewriting, printing, photostating, photographing, photocopying, transmitting by electronic mail or facsimile, and every other means of recording upon any tangible thing any form of communication or representation.

RESPONSE REQUIREMENTS

SECTION 7922.535. RESPONSE TO REQUESTS (AMENDED 2025)

(a) Each public agency, upon a request for a copy of records, shall, within 10 days from receipt of the request, determine whether the request, in whole or in part, seeks copies of disclosable public records in the possession of the agency and shall promptly notify the person making the request of the determination and the reasons therefor.

(b) UNUSUAL CIRCUMSTANCES EXTENSION (AMENDED 2025 - AB 370):
In unusual circumstances, the time limit prescribed in subdivision (a) may be extended by written notice by the head of the agency or his or her designee to the requester setting forth the reasons for the extension and the date on which a determination is expected to be dispatched. No such notice shall specify a date that would result in an extension for more than 14 days.

EXPANDED UNUSUAL CIRCUMSTANCES (AB 370 - EFFECTIVE JULY 2025):
"Unusual circumstances" now includes:
(1) The need to search for and collect the requested records from field facilities or other establishments that are separate from the office processing the request
(2) The need to search for, collect, and appropriately examine a voluminous amount of separate and distinct records that are demanded in a single request
(3) The need for consultation, which shall be conducted with all practicable speed, with another agency having substantial interest in the determination of the request or among two or more components of the agency having substantial subject matter interest therein
(4) The need to compile data, write programming language or a computer program, or construct a computer report to extract data
(5) The inability of the agency, because of a cyberattack, to access its electronic servers or systems in order to search for and obtain a record that the agency believes is responsive to the request and is maintained on the servers or systems in an electronic format

CYBERATTACK PROVISIONS (NEW 2025 - AB 370):
When a public agency experiences a cyberattack that prevents access to electronic systems:
- Agency may invoke 14-day extension for affected records
- Must provide written notice explaining the nature of the cyberattack impact
- Must provide alternative formats or methods if available
- Extension applies only to records maintained on affected systems
- Agency must provide records from unaffected systems within normal timeframes

FEES AND CHARGES

SECTION 7922.530. FEES
(a) Nothing in this chapter shall be construed to require disclosure of records the disclosure of which is prohibited by state or federal law, including, but not limited to, provisions of the Evidence Code relating to privilege.

(b) Except with respect to public records exempt from disclosure by express provisions of law, each state and local agency, upon a request for a copy of records, shall, within 10 days from receipt of the request, determine whether the request, in whole or in part, seeks copies of disclosable public records in the possession of the agency and shall promptly notify the person making the request of the determination and the reasons therefor.

STANDARD FEES:
- Direct costs of duplication: Actual cost
- Computer programming time: Actual cost
- Search time: Generally not chargeable unless voluminous
- Electronic format: Cost of medium

EXEMPTIONS

SECTION 7927.000. EXEMPTIONS GENERALLY
Nothing in this chapter shall be construed to require disclosure of records that are any of the following:

(1) Preliminary drafts, notes, or interagency or intraagency memoranda that are not retained by the public agency in the ordinary course of business
(2) Records pertaining to pending litigation involving the public agency
(3) Personnel, medical, or similar files where disclosure would constitute an unwarranted invasion of personal privacy
(4) Records the disclosure of which is prohibited by state or federal law
(5) Law enforcement investigatory records in specified circumstances

PRIVACY PROTECTIONS (ENHANCED 2025 - AB 1785):

ELECTED OFFICIALS' PERSONAL INFORMATION:
The home address and assessor parcel number of elected officials are exempt from disclosure when:
- The official makes a written request for confidentiality
- The request is filed with the appropriate public agency
- The exemption applies to current and former elected officials
- Family members residing at the same address are also protected

This exemption applies to:
- State legislators and constitutional officers
- Local elected officials (mayors, city council members, county supervisors, school board members)
- Judicial officers
- Other elected positions as defined by law

PEACE OFFICER INFORMATION (ENHANCED 2025):

SECTION 832.7 COORDINATION:
Records related to peace officer investigations held by POST Commission:
- Investigation records are not public while held by POST
- POST must forward requests to originating law enforcement agency
- Originating agency must respond under standard CPRA timeframes
- Does not limit public access to misconduct records under Penal Code 832.7

ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES

SECTION 7923.000. ENFORCEMENT
Any person may institute proceedings for injunctive or declarative relief or writ of mandate in any court of competent jurisdiction to enforce the provisions of this chapter. The court shall award reasonable attorney's fees and other litigation costs reasonably incurred in any case under this section in which the plaintiff has substantially prevailed.

SECTION 7924.000. CRIMINAL PENALTIES
Each member of a legislative body who attends a meeting of that body where action is taken in violation of any provision of this chapter, and where the member intends to deprive the public of information to which the member knows or has reason to know the public is entitled, is guilty of a misdemeanor.

RECENT LEGISLATIVE AMENDMENTS (2025)

ASSEMBLY BILL 134 (2025):
Investigation Records Held by Commission on Peace Officer Standards and Training
- POST investigation records are not public records while in POST custody
- POST forwards requests to originating law enforcement agencies
- Does not impede public access under Penal Code Section 832.7
- Temporary provision expires January 1, 2027

ASSEMBLY BILL 370 (CARRILLO) - SIGNED JULY 14, 2025:
California Public Records Act: Cyberattacks
- Adds cyberattack as "unusual circumstance" for response extensions
- Allows 14-day extension when cyberattack prevents system access
- Requires written explanation of cyberattack impact
- Sponsored by City of Hesperia, supported by various local agencies

ASSEMBLY BILL 1785 (2025):
Enhanced privacy protections for elected officials' home addresses and assessor parcel numbers

PENDING LEGISLATION (2025):

ASSEMBLY BILL 541:
Proposes creation of California Public Records Act Ombudsperson
- Would provide mediation services for CPRA disputes
- Currently under consideration, pending appropriation
- Would be housed within existing government structure

ATTORNEY GENERAL GUIDANCE:

JUDICIAL INTERPRETATIONS (2024-2025):

Recent court decisions have addressed:
- Scope of cyberattack exemptions and documentation requirements
- Application of privacy protections to elected officials
- Electronic format requirements and costs
- Timelines during emergency declarations

BEST PRACTICES FOR AGENCIES:

RESPONSE PROCEDURES:
- Acknowledge requests promptly (within 10 days)
- Provide detailed explanations for any denials
- Offer to meet and confer on unclear requests
- Maintain detailed logs of requests and responses
- Train staff on current legal requirements

CYBERSECURITY CONSIDERATIONS:
- Document cyberattack impacts thoroughly
- Provide alternative access methods when possible
- Coordinate with IT security teams on disclosure timing
- Maintain backup systems for critical public records

TECHNOLOGY AND ELECTRONIC RECORDS:

ELECTRONIC FORMAT REQUIREMENTS:
- Provide records in electronic format when requested and available
- Format should be commonly used and machine-readable
- May charge actual cost of programming for database extracts
- Consider accessibility requirements for disabled requesters

SOCIAL MEDIA AND DIGITAL COMMUNICATIONS:
- Official social media accounts are subject to CPRA
- Text messages and emails on personal devices may be public records
- Metadata may be subject to disclosure
- Retention policies must comply with public records requirements

LOCAL AGENCY VARIATIONS:

MUNICIPAL POLICIES:
- Cities may adopt more liberal disclosure policies
- Some provide online request portals
- Local fee schedules within statutory limits
- Enhanced public access initiatives

COUNTY PROCEDURES:
- Centralized records management systems
- Specialized procedures for law enforcement records
- Coordination with state agencies
- Public access to meetings and documents

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:

SCHOOL DISTRICTS:
- Student records have enhanced privacy protections
- FERPA compliance requirements
- Special procedures for personnel records
- Athletic and activities records considerations

HEALTHCARE AGENCIES:
- HIPAA compliance coordination
- Patient privacy protections
- Public health emergency provisions
- Medical research confidentiality

RECENT TRENDS AND ISSUES (2024-2025):

EMERGING CHALLENGES:
- Ransomware and cyberattack response procedures
- Artificial intelligence and automated decision-making records
- Cloud storage and third-party vendor records
- Remote work and digital communication records

LITIGATION DEVELOPMENTS:
- Increased focus on timely responses during emergencies
- Privacy vs. transparency balance in digital age
- Commercial use vs. public interest determinations
- Attorney fee awards in enforcement actions

COMPLIANCE RECOMMENDATIONS:

FOR PUBLIC AGENCIES:
- Implement comprehensive records management systems
- Provide regular CPRA training for all staff
- Develop clear policies for electronic records
- Establish partnerships with IT security teams
- Create public-friendly request processes

FOR REQUESTERS:
- Submit specific, clearly defined requests
- Be prepared for reasonable processing times
- Consider electronic formats to reduce costs
- Work cooperatively with agencies when possible
- Understand exemptions and privacy limitations

KEY 2025 CHANGES SUMMARY:
1. Cyberattack added as "unusual circumstance" (AB 370)
2. Enhanced privacy protections for elected officials (AB 1785)
3. Clarified POST Commission records procedures (AB 134)
4. Proposed CPRA Ombudsperson position (AB 541 - pending)
5. Improved coordination between CPRA and peace officer records laws
