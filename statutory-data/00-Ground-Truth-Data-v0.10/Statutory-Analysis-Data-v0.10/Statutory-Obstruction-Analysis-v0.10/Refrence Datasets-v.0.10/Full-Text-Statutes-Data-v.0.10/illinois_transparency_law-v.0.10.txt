ILLINOIS FREEDOM OF INFORMATION ACT (FOIA)
Illinois Compiled Statutes Chapter 5, Section 140
Effective Date: Original enactment 1984; multiple amendments

OFFICIAL CITATION: 5 ILCS 140/1 et seq.

PURPOSE AND SCOPE:
"It is declared to be the public policy of the State of Illinois that all persons are entitled to full and complete information regarding the affairs of government and the official acts of those who represent them as public officials and public employees."

KEY DEFINITIONS (5 ILCS 140/2):
- "Public body": All legislative, executive, administrative, or advisory bodies of the State
- "Public records": All records, reports, forms, writings, letters, memoranda, books, papers, maps, photographs, cards, tapes, recordings, electronic data processing records, recorded information and all other documentary materials pertaining to the transaction of public business
- "Person": Any individual, corporation, partnership, firm, organization or association, acting individually or as a group

PRESUMPTION OF OPENNESS:
All records in the custody or possession of a public body are presumed to be open to inspection or copying.

REQUEST REQUIREMENTS (5 ILCS 140/3):
- Request must be in writing (including electronic submission)
- Can be submitted via personal delivery, mail, fax, or email
- No standard form required
- Must clearly identify the records sought
- Public bodies cannot require purpose statement (except commercial requests)
- Must include requester's contact information

RESPONSE TIMEFRAMES (5 ILCS 140/3):
Standard Requests:
- 5 business days to respond (grant, deny, or extend)
- May extend up to additional 5 business days with written notice

Commercial Requests:
- 21 working days to respond
- May extend up to additional 10 business days with written notice

Voluminous Requests:
- Additional time allowed for requests requiring compilation or extensive search

FEES (5 ILCS 140/6):
Standard Fees:
- First 50 pages of black and white copies: FREE
- Additional black and white copies: $0.15 per page
- Color copies: actual cost
- Electronic records: actual cost of medium
- Certified copies: $1.00 per document plus copy fees
- Search and review time may be charged for commercial requests

Fee Waivers:
- Available when disclosure serves the public interest
- When requestor's primary purpose is to access information for publication/broadcast

EXEMPTIONS (5 ILCS 140/7):
Records exempt from disclosure include:
- Information specifically prohibited from disclosure by federal or state law
- Private information (personal, medical, financial details)
- Personal records maintained by public bodies
- Records of law enforcement investigations
- Preliminary drafts, notes, recommendations, memoranda
- Trade secrets and commercial information
- Information received in confidence
- Test questions and answers for academic or licensing examinations
- Library circulation records
- Architect's and engineer's plans for buildings until construction completed

ENFORCEMENT AND APPEALS (5 ILCS 140/11):
Administrative Review:
- Request review by Public Access Counselor within 60 days of denial
- PAC provides binding determination (with limited exceptions)

Judicial Review:
- File suit in circuit court for injunctive or declaratory relief
- Court may award reasonable attorney's fees and costs
- Civil penalties up to $5,000 for willful and intentional violations

PENALTIES (5 ILCS 140/11):
- Civil penalties for willful violations: $2,500-$5,000
- Attorney's fees and costs available for prevailing requesters
- Disciplinary action possible for public officials

SPECIAL PROVISIONS:
Electronic Records:
- Public bodies must provide records in electronic format when requested
- Cannot require specific software to access records

Privacy Protection:
- Must redact personal information before disclosure
- Balancing test for privacy vs. public interest

Meeting Records:
- Audio recordings of open meetings must be made available
- Minutes and recordings subject to FOIA

RELATED LAWS:
- Open Meetings Act (5 ILCS 120/) - governs public meeting access
- Local Records Act - governs record retention
- State Records Act - governs state record management

NOTABLE FEATURES:
- Free first 50 pages of copies
- Strong presumption toward disclosure  
- Independent Public Access Counselor for appeals
- Binding administrative determinations
- Attorney fee provisions encourage compliance
- Electronic access requirements
- Civil penalty structure

RECENT UPDATES:
Check current Illinois Compiled Statutes for latest amendments and court interpretations.

REQUESTING TIPS:
- Be specific in describing records sought
- Consider requesting electronic format to reduce costs
- Use proper FOIA contact for each agency
- Allow adequate response time
- Appeal denials to Public Access Counselor before court action
